To maintain a current Pilot License, pilots are required to do regular training, but more important a good pilot need to stay current on the necessary skills and knowledge to operate an aircraft safely within the demands of commercial aviation.

Flying and aircraft demands a high level of proficiency in various maneuvers, emergency procedures, workload management etc. An LPC evaluates a pilot’s ability to handle routine and unexpected situations, promoting a culture of continuous improvement and preparedness for and scenario.

The LPC should contribute to maintaining a high level of safety within the aviation industry. By systematically assessing and reinforcing a pilot’s skills, airlines and regulatory bodies endure that those entrusted with the responsibility of passenger safety are consistently capable and qualified.

The LPC is a crucial component of a pilot’s ongoing training and assessment. It not only ensures compliance with industry standards but also guarantees that pilots remain proficient, adaptable, and capable of handling the dynamic challenges of commercial aviation, ultimately enhancing the safety and reliability of air travel.
